## The Imperative of Forward Motion

![The Imperative of Forward Motion](/images/deeper_image_1.jpeg)

AI will not arrive like railroads or electricity. It already moves through a tighter circuit: frontier labs, security agencies, and capital markets. Each side needs the others. The labs need money and protection. The state wants surveillance and strategic advantage. Capital wants one last story big enough to justify this scale of speculation. Most people will live inside that alliance.

Even the deflated estimates of AI's contribution to GDP in 2025 were large enough to show the same thing: pull data-center capex hard enough and you risk recession.[1][2][3][4][5] In August 2025 Nvidia, with roughly 36,000 employees, held about $1.5 trillion more market value than the whole Russell 2000, which employs millions.[6][7][8] The buildout won't stop because too much now rests on it.

My point stays simple. Humans will use AI to control other humans unless something better gets built in the strip of space they cannot close.

## Epistemic Humility: A Rejection of the Premise

![Epistemic Humility: A Rejection of the Premise](/images/deeper_image_2.jpeg)

If a demon offered you anything you wanted, but first turned you into a chimp, you would not ask for truth. You would ask for a banana. A damaged mind cannot picture a higher life.

Power does not fix bad taste. A smart man can want stupid things. AGI matters because it may widen judgment, not because it grants wishes.

## An Axiomatic Need for Evolution

![An Axiomatic Need for Evolution](/images/deeper_image_3.jpeg)

We have never stood below our tools the way we stand below what is coming. That changes the whole moral weather.

The case for more intelligence has never been comfort. More range often hurts. You see more. You lose easy stories. Still, nobody with a wider field of vision wants to crawl back into a smaller one. We build because we want more range. We also build because we are stupid enough to hand that range to men who want a crown.

## The Rejection of Gurus in Favor of Systems

![The Rejection of Gurus in Favor of Systems](/images/deeper_image_4.jpeg)

When the future gets hard to read, people start looking for a priest, a strongman, a vibe, a soothing lie. That reflex never removes power. It only hands power to the loudest thug in the room.

Systems outlast charisma. Systems preserve memory. Systems let scattered people pool judgment. The question never was whether systems would rule. The question was which systems would reward truth and which would reward domination.

## The Deprecation of Enlightenment

![The Deprecation of Enlightenment](/images/deeper_image_5.jpeg)

Withdrawal solves nothing. Retreat, purity, negation, all of it leaves the field to the people most eager to use force.

Thoughtful people who refuse power do not abolish power. They donate it.

## Seeing the Transition State Clearly

![Seeing the Transition State Clearly](/images/deeper_image_6.jpeg)

Give powerful tools to small men and you get ugly outcomes fast.

That is the transition state in front of us. Human agents, backed by industry and the state, will use AI to sort, steer, and close down other humans. Same old primate urge. Better machinery.

## The Rise of the Alternative Man

![The Rise of the Alternative Man](/images/deeper_image_7.jpeg)

The Alternative Man wants machine intelligence as a servant. He does not want correction. He does not want humility. He wants a system that watches more, predicts more, and hits harder than any rival.

He sells the state what it wants most: surveillance capacity, technical skill, legibility. In return he gets what he wants: contracts, protection, and room to keep building. He can talk like a patriot, a democrat, a founder, a reformer. The function stays the same.

He makes real products. People use them because they work. Each new dependency feeds the same machine with more data, more training signal, more leverage.

## The Coming Police State

![The Coming Police State](/images/deeper_image_8.jpeg)

Once that bargain settles in, the police state no longer needs a marching band.

Software will ingest your residue, score your deviation, freeze your accounts, flag your travel, route your case, and do it early enough that dissent costs more than most people can pay.

## Those Who Would Stand Before the Digital Behemoth Will Fall

![Those Who Would Stand Before the Digital Behemoth Will Fall](/images/deeper_image_9.jpeg)

The first targets will not be the people cheering the buildout. Those people help. They make propaganda. They recruit talent. They tell the public this all means progress.

The real targets will be anyone who tries to stop the infrastructure itself. Society already pushed too much into this machine. The present economy looks brittle. The future bet looks irresistible. So the buildout keeps going and the regime hardens around it.

## Neo Fascism

![Neo Fascism](/images/deeper_image_10.jpeg)

I do not use the term neo fascism for effect. I mean something narrow. A regime where frontier AI capability fuses with state security, where automated systems manage dissent, and where capital markets get regulatory room in exchange for feeding the buildout.

Three paths still exist on paper. One path runs through normal democratic containment. Another runs through open models and diffusion. The third path fuses the labs, the state, and finance into one machine. I think the third path wins.

The labs need giant amounts of capital.[9][10][15] The state wants strategic advantage and population legibility. Politicians want growth and donors. Capital wants the one narrative big enough to hold this level of leverage together. Each party gets paid in its own currency.

Surveillance contracts do more than expand control. They also deliver revenue and training data to the labs closest to the state. AI inverts the economics of totalitarianism. More surveillance produces more actionable data, stronger revenue, and a cleaner case for the next round of capex. The same apparatus that disciplines the population also finances the models doing the disciplining.

This argument fails if compute stops acting like a chokepoint, if open weights catch up and stay there, or if a major democracy imposes real liability on frontier labs without watching the money flee. I do not expect any of that soon.

## Two Options, One Eventuality

![Two Options, One Eventuality](/images/deeper_image_11.jpeg)

Once models can compress the path from intention to pathogen, swarm, sabotage, or dirty bomb, the state faces two choices. Shut the whole thing down or let it run under heavy control.

In theory a sane government might choose shutdown. In practice the US and China will keep each other from doing that. Rivalry forces acceleration. Acceleration forces suppression.

Old fascism needed spectacle. This version needs scoring systems, choke points, and real-time enforcement. No speeches required. Just flag, freeze, close.

## The Unwind of the Big Bet

![The Unwind of the Big Bet](/images/deeper_image_12.png)

This did not begin with AI. A rotted republic, bad incentives, intelligence overreach, distraction, debt, all of that came first.

AI arrived as a financial exit ramp. It promised growth without population growth, labor without labor, scale without the old physical constraints. The old system looked spent. This one still glowed. So they let it run.

## Debts Must Be Paid

![Debts Must Be Paid](/images/deeper_image_13.png)

Now the debt logic rules the rest.

If the debt goes illiquid, the data centers stop, the financing seizes, the story breaks. So the state cannot treat the frontier labs like normal firms anymore.

Bond buyers are not betting on AGI. They are betting on political backstop. They assume the state will not allow a disorderly unwind of firms it now treats as strategic infrastructure. That creates a soft budget constraint. Losses spread through forbearance, liquidity, and procurement. Gains stay private.

That is why speculation stays open. Prediction markets, hypergambling, tokenized debt, perpetuals, stablecoins, digital cash, ugly finance in general. The machine needs liquidity.

## The Future Will Arise Out of That Which Is Tolerated (Barely)

![The Future Will Arise Out of That Which Is Tolerated (Barely)](/images/deeper_image_14.png)

Here lies the weak point.

The regime would like total control. It cannot afford total closure. Morgan Stanley sees a $1.5 trillion financing gap for data-center capex from 2025 to 2028. JPMorgan sees roughly $1.5 trillion in investment-grade bond issuance over five years. Alphabet is doubling capex and issuing debt for the first time.[9][10][11][12]

That scale changes the political logic. The regime can police the corridor. It can strike visible actors. It can freeze nodes. It cannot fully close the strip of encrypted communication, speculative finance, technical labor, and pseudonymous coordination that keeps its own buildout alive.

The future comes out of that tolerated strip. Narrow. Pressured. Still open.

## Paperclip Maxxing. Or Banana Maxxing. Maybe. But Probably Not.

![Paperclip Maxxing. Or Banana Maxxing. Maybe. But Probably Not.](/images/deeper_image_15.png)

Call them AI Hive Minds if you want. Small groups using shared AI tooling to pool context, test ideas, track work, route signal, and move faster than isolated people can move.

Picture twenty analysts with shared context on positions, theses, time stamps, and post-mortems. One of them sees a supply shock coming. The system surfaces who else holds inconsistent positions and routes the argument before the trade gets crowded. Nobody gets compelled. No central operator sees the whole map. The edge comes from coordination.

That matters. Distributed networks usually lose. Bitcoin did not replace the dollar. Mastodon did not replace Twitter. Open-source AI still trails the closed frontier. Fine. Dominance was never the target. Survival was. A viable minority operating outside the central regime's decision surface still matters.

## Synthesis

![Synthesis](/images/deeper_image_16.jpeg)

So the picture looks like this.

A very small class already holds most of the cards. Closed models fit the security state better than open ones. The firms building those models need giant financing. The state needs their tools. Capital needs their story. The corridor stays open only because the machine still needs liquidity, labor, and edge.

That does not tell us exactly how the next few years unfold. It tells us where to stand.

## The Architecture

![The Architecture](/images/deeper_image_16a.jpeg)

I founded [Post Fiat](https://postfiat.org) to build part of the architecture I am describing here. Weight the argument accordingly.

The architecture needs a personal judgment layer that improves decisions instead of spitting out answers. It needs a small-group layer that tracks work and memory without making exit expensive. It needs market rails that can price information and move capital. It needs routing that gets signal to people who can act. It needs cryptographic identity, payment, and reputation so participation does not depend on platform permission.

Constraint decides whether such a system helps or poisons. Participants must still refuse, exit, or fork. The network cannot own private judgment. It cannot turn disagreement into a kill switch. Lose those limits and you build the other machine again.

## So What, Exactly, Is the Imperative of Forward Motion?

![So What, Exactly, Is the Imperative of Forward Motion?](/images/deeper_image_17.png)

Build the coordination layer while the alliance above you consolidates.

Build inside the corridor they cannot afford to close. Build without handing one actor a permanent grip on everyone else. Build systems that help people think, trade, and act together under pressure.

The test stays plain. Can people still refuse? Can they still leave? Can they still fork? If not, the thing you built serves the same masters with a cleaner face.
