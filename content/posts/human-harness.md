The Task Node was science fiction when models were toys. It is not science fiction anymore.

I built an interactive presentation for the next phase of the experiment: two weeks of running life through the Task Node as aggressively as possible. Context document, task execution, evidence, rewards, Module Chat, Telegram coaching, multiplayer pressure, network context, and the Hive Mind planning layer all treated as one operating system for human action.

[Enter the Human Harness presentation](/human-harness/)

The thesis is simple: if AI systems can build most things, execution by itself is less of a moat. The edge moves to deciding what to do, exposing your real execution history to the system, letting the system rewrite your strategy when reality contradicts it, and then actually doing the task it gives you.

The browser is the cockpit. Telegram is the field terminal. The full-auto experiment only matters if the loop follows you away from the desk.
